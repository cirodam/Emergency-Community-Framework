# Legal Structure

## Overview

Each ECF community should be structured as a **worker cooperative** — a member-owned entity where governance follows the one-member-one-vote principle and surplus is distributed by labor contribution, not capital. This maps directly onto the pool/assembly governance model in the software.

Land is held separately in a **community land trust (CLT)** to protect it from sale, debt seizure, or individual extraction. A thin **cooperative federation** holds shared infrastructure (software, services) across multiple communities without having authority over any of them.

---

## Community Entity: Worker Cooperative

### Why a cooperative, not a nonprofit or LLC

| Form | Problem |
|---|---|
| Nonprofit | Board legally controls assets; members have no equity; restricts distributions |
| LLC (default) | No inherent democratic requirements; majority interest holder can dominate |
| Worker cooperative | One member one vote; patronage-based surplus; maps onto pool/assembly model |

### Recommended forms

- **Worker cooperative corporation** — available in most states under cooperative statutes
- **Limited Cooperative Association (LCA)** — more flexible; best statutes in Colorado and Wyoming
- Start as an **LLC with a democratic operating agreement** for speed and low cost, then convert to a formal cooperative once membership justifies it

### Key governance principles to encode in the operating agreement

- One member, one vote — no plural voting by capital contribution
- Membership is earned by labor participation, not purchase
- Surplus distributed by patronage (hours contributed) not equity held
- No member may hold a controlling stake
- Dissolution proceeds distributed to members and/or the CLT, never to outside investors

---

## Land: Community Land Trust (CLT)

### Why a separate CLT

Land is the most vulnerable community asset. Title held by the cooperative can be seized for debts, sold by a bad actor, or lost in litigation. A CLT:

- Holds land permanently — it can never be sold for individual profit
- Issues long-term ground leases to members for homes and plots
- Keeps housing permanently affordable as the community grows (resale price formulas cap individual gain)
- Is legally separate from the cooperative, so operational disputes don't threaten the land

### Relationship to the cooperative

The CLT and the cooperative are separate legal entities with overlapping membership. The cooperative leases land from the CLT. The CLT's board should include community members elected by the cooperative assembly, ensuring alignment without merger.

## Internal Currency

The internal currency is a **closed-loop labor credit system**. It is:

- Not redeemable for US dollars, ever
- Not a security, commodity, or money service
- A ledger of labor obligations within the community

### Why non-convertibility is the right design

- Keeps the system outside money transmission law (no FinCEN registration, no state banking licenses)
- Prevents wealth extraction by outside capital
- Prevents speculation — no exchange rate to manipulate
- Enforces participation — the currency only has value inside the community

### External trade

The community will need to acquire some goods externally (specialized equipment, raw materials). The correct design:

- The **cooperative entity** trades externally, not individual members
- External dollars flow into a **common pool** governed by the assembly
- Individual members never receive dollars through the community — only the cooperative entity transacts in dollars
- This closes the legal loop and prevents the common pool from becoming a back-channel for currency conversion

## Federation Layer

When multiple communities federate, a thin umbrella entity holds shared infrastructure without governing member communities.

### Recommended form

A **cooperative federation** or **nonprofit** that:
- Holds the software, shared services, and brand
- Has no authority over member community governance
- Is funded by member community contributions (flat fee or per-member)
- Is governed by delegates from member communities

### Models

- **Federation of Southern Cooperatives** — US cooperative federation, relevant model
- **Mondragon Corporation** — large-scale cooperative federation (cautionary note: Mondragon has drifted from cooperative principles at scale)

---

## Legal Sequencing

1. **Form an LLC** with a democratic operating agreement — fast, cheap, sufficient for early organizing
2. **Convert to a worker cooperative** once membership is stable and the operating model is proven
3. **Set up a CLT** as soon as land acquisition is on the table — do not acquire land in the cooperative's name
4. **Federate** under a thin umbrella once multiple communities are operating
