# Governance Motion Model

## Two modes of governance

This community has two distinct modes of decision-making, and the software treats them differently.

### Referenda
Fully async, fully online. No meeting required.

A proposal goes up, a deliberation period runs, and members vote from wherever they are over days or weeks. The system handles the entire lifecycle — timers, vote collection, threshold checking, and resolution — without a facilitator.

### Assembly and leader pools
Meet in person (or synchronously). The software supports the meeting; it does not run it.

The agenda page is a **meeting docket**, not a voting machine. Before the meeting, members read what is on the agenda. During the meeting, a steward acts as clerk — advancing items, recording outcomes, triggering any downstream referenda that result. After the meeting, the record is permanent and visible to the full community.

---

## Motion lifecycle

### Referenda motions (software-driven)

| Stage | Description |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | Written by any member; not yet visible on the agenda |
| **On agenda** | Submitted for deliberation; deliberation timer starts |
| **Deliberation** | Open for comment; minimum period before voting opens |
| **Voting** | Comments close; votes collected until deadline or threshold reached |
| **Resolved** | Passed or failed; permanently recorded |

### Assembly / pool motions (clerk-driven)

| Stage | Description |
|---|---|
| **Proposed** | Added to the docket before the meeting |
| **Discussed** | Marked by the steward/clerk during the meeting |
| **Voted** | Outcome recorded by the clerk (pass / fail / referred) |
| **Referred** | Sent to referendum or to another body |
| **Resolved** | Recorded; any triggered actions (e.g. referenda) initiated |

---

## What is dropped from Robert's Rules

- No seconds required to bring a motion forward
- No points of order or procedural motions
- No tabling (a motion can be withdrawn)
- No formal speaking queue — deliberation is an async comment thread for referenda; in-room for assembly/pool meetings

## What is kept

- Any member can propose an amendment to a motion during deliberation; the amendment is itself a motion that must resolve before the parent votes
- The body decides which vote threshold applies (simple majority / supermajority / near consensus) when accepting the motion
- Quorum: full membership for referenda; seated assembly members for assembly votes

---

## Assembly agenda page

**Before the meeting:** ordered docket of items with descriptions; members read to prepare.

**During the meeting:** a steward (clerk) advances each item through the lifecycle from a phone or laptop. The app is the record-keeper, not the chair.

**After the meeting:** outcomes are visible to the full community. Any referenda triggered by the meeting go live immediately.

---

## Leader pool meetings

Pool meetings are generally working sessions. Formal votes are rare. The same docket model applies, but most items will resolve as decisions recorded by the clerk rather than formal votes. If a pool decision requires assembly or referendum ratification, the clerk initiates that motion before closing the item.
